About me

I am an undergraduate sophomore at the University of Pennsylvania, where I am studying Computer Science and Biology and pursuing an Accelerated Master’s degree in Robotics. My passion lies in the intersection of technology and its impact on people’s lives. Through my coursework and personal projects, I have developed proficiency in software development frameworks and programming languages. I have also built big data analytics and machine learning pipelines, as well as parallel and distributed computing environments to efficiently manage large computational costs.

Beyond my tech interests, I am deeply passionate about life sciences. As an undergraduate bioinformatician at Penn’s School of Medicine, I have applied my programming and data science expertise to multiple bioinformatic projects, solving complex biological problems with data analytics. My experience in the lab has highlighted numerous pain points in the life science workflow, inspiring me to create projects that merge life science with technology.

I have developed NeuraLIVE and Vision4Science, each addressing critical steps in biological data analysis and experimental methodology communication. Currently, I am working on SciSketch, a generative AI-powered tool designed to simplify the life science diagramming process.
